I am an PhD student in the Department of Earth and Space Sciences" and the Polar Science Center at the University of Washington. I use satellite images to measure changes in coastal glacier extent in Greenland and Alaska, to better understand how these glaciers are responding to climate change.

I am an experienced science communicator and currently serve on the board of the Engage science communication program at UW. I have shared stories about my research and career path in grade schools, pubs, and more.

Prior to pursuing glaciology research, I earned an M.S. in Earth and Atmospheric Sciences, during which I studied the behavior of unsteady explosive volcanic eruptions using computational fluid dynamics models. I have a general background in geology and physics, and my previous project experience includes sea ice optics, meteorite petrology, cosmogenic nuclide dating, and paleontology/paleobotany.

My current projects include: seasonal and multi-decadal variability of Greenland outlet glaciers, with Ian Joughin of UW-APL-PSC; role of glacier variability for polar bear habitat in SE Greenland, with Kristin Laidre of UW-APL-PSC and Twila Moon of CU-Boulder; cataloguing glacier retreat in Kenai Fjords National Park, with Deb Kurtz of the U.S. National Park Service; and developing icepicks, a comprehensive database of glacier terminus data, with Ginny Catania and Sophie Goliber of UT-Austin and others.
