Teaching

I taught courses and laboratory sections at a range of levels and both within and outside my primary expertise in glaciology and geophysics. Although my graduate studies were fully funded, I sought opportunities to gain teaching experience when possible, both in and out of the classroom.

University of Washington

ESS 431: Principles of Glaciology
I TA'd this upper-division glaciology course for two weeks as part of an experimental distributed TA model during the COVID-19 pandemic. During synchronous online discussion sections I helped students think through conceptual questions and break down quantitative problem sets.
CENV 500: Communicating Science to the Public Effectively
I taught a graduate-level course in public science communication, which heavily emphasized group discussions and active learning. In addition to lecturing and leading discussions, I organized a series of expert guest lectures from other experienced science communicators. I guided students through developing and presenting twenty-minute public lectures about their research, and managed a pivot to a virtual format when our speaker series was interrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic.
ESS 101: Introduction to Geology and Societal Impacts
I alternated teaching lab sections and giving lectures in the summer section of this introductory geology course for non-majors, and I led a field trip to introduce students to the geology of the Columbia River Plateau.
ESS 213: Evolution of the Earth
I taught and graded twice-weekly laboratory sections for this introductory series course for ESS majors, which introduces concepts in sedimentology, stratigraphy, and paleontology. I also assisted with two local field trips, and with designing and grading exams and term projects.

Georgia Institute of Technology

Gwinnett County Math and Science Partnership
I provided scientific expertise and occasional lectures for a science education endorsement program for K-5 teachers in Gwinnett County, which was taught in partnership with Georgia Institute of Technology.
EAS 2600: Earth Processes
I taught and graded laboratory sections for an introductory geology course for majors and non-majors, and assisted with proctoring and grading exams.
